Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 30, 2024

This Privacy Policy ("Privacy Policy") describes how Virtue: Digital Detox ("we," "us," "our," or "Operator") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use the Virtue: Digital Detox mobile application ("Application") and related services ("Service").

The protection of your privacy and personal data is our highest priority. We process personal data in strict compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and the Information Technology Act, 2000 and its rules.

By using our Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, and we will notify you of any changes. The latest version is always available within the Application and on our website.

1. General Information

Under applicable data protection laws, we are the controller of your personal data. This means we determine the purposes and means of processing your personal information in connection with providing the Service.

We process your personal data only when:

  • It is necessary for the performance of our contract with you (i.e., providing the Service)
  • We have a legitimate interest in processing the data
  • We are required to do so by law
  • You have given us your explicit consent

Personal data is processed both manually and through automated means to ensure the proper functioning of the Service and to maintain our internal procedures.

2. Information Collection and Use

Virtue: Digital Detox does not collect or store any Personal Information except data needed for optional features such as account creation, progress backup, and app synchronization. These features are entirely voluntary, and you may use the core Service without creating an account.

All usage statistics, blocking configurations, and app data are stored locally on your device and are not accessible by us or our affiliates. We do not have access to information about which apps you block or how you use the Service.

Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services for analytics, advertising, and crash reporting. These services are governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Google Play Services - For app distribution and core functionality
  • Firebase Analytics - For anonymous usage analytics
  • Firebase Crashlytics - For crash reporting and app stability
  • AdMob - For serving advertisements (if applicable)

3. Purposes of Personal Data Processing

We may process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Performance of contract: To provide the Service and fulfill our contractual obligations to you
  • Legal compliance: To comply with applicable laws and regulations
  • Legitimate interests: To protect our rights, improve the Service, and create anonymized statistics
  • Marketing communications: To send you information about the Service via email or push notifications (only with your consent)
  • Customer support: To respond to your inquiries and provide assistance

4.2 Technical Data

When you use the Service, we may automatically collect:

  • Device information (model, operating system version)
  • IP address
  • App version and configuration
  • Crash logs and error reports (anonymous)

5. Installed Apps Inventory Access

The Service requires access to your device's list of installed applications to provide its core functionality, including:

  • Allowing you to select which apps to block
  • Displaying usage statistics for blocked apps
  • Enforcing blocking rules you configure

This data is used solely on your device and is never transmitted to our servers or third parties. We do not collect, store, or have access to information about which apps you have installed or which apps you choose to block.

6. Usage Accessibility Services

The Service uses Android Accessibility Services to detect when you attempt to open blocked applications and to enforce your blocking preferences. This permission is essential for the core functionality of the app.

We do not use Accessibility Services to collect, transmit, or store any data about your device usage. All blocking logic operates locally on your device.

7. Log Data

In case of an error in the Application, we collect Log Data through third-party services. This may include:

  • Device IP address
  • Device name and model
  • Operating system version
  • App configuration at the time of error
  • Time and date of the error
  • Other diagnostic statistics

This data is used solely to improve the Service and ensure reliability. It does not contain personally identifiable information and cannot be used to identify individual users.

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data against:

  • Unauthorized access or disclosure
  • Accidental loss or destruction
  • Malicious attacks
  • Unlawful processing

Your account password is encrypted and stored securely. We recommend using a strong, unique password and not sharing your login credentials with anyone.

However, please note that no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your personal data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

All third-party service providers are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the purposes we specify.
